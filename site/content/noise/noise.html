"Perlin noise is a type of gradient noise developed by Ken Perlin in 1983. It has many uses, including but not limited to: procedurally generating terrain, applying pseudo-random changes to a variable, and assisting in the creation of image textures. It is most commonly implemented in two, three, or four dimensions, but can be defined for any number of dimensions." - Wikipedia.org

What is noise?

Noise are pseudo-randomly generated values which are affected by their neighbor values. Usually these values are normalized, which means they may have a possible value between zero and one. Noise is most commonly utilized in the generation of heightmaps in open-world-games but can also be used to generate any kind of pseudo-random pattern.

Perlin Noise used in the generation of terrain

Perlin Noise

Parameter Usage
Octaves Octaves are different graphs generated with perlin noise which are combined into one to form a more natural or smooth graph.
Frequency The frequency is the amount of values generated on the graph. This may also help provide a bigger sample number.
Amplitude The amplitude is the highest value reached by the graph at any point. This may be used to control the values that are being generated, such as for a heightmap.
